A Fresh Start!

To be honest, I'm relieved that I dropped the Space Invaders Demo. After the first week, it wasn't even trying to be something similar to Space Invaders, and I really didn't understand where I was going to begin with.

With that experience, however, I've learned a lot, and I'll be applying it to this new demo to test out how my ideal cooking system in a game looks like. To sum up what I learned:

  • Ignore the game mechanics that aren't important to the identity/core of your game.
  • Try to plan ahead to so you don't have to go back and change your code (it saves a LOT of time).
  • Don't worry too much about game balancing, you can always save it for later.
  • When you're making things, don't try to immediately tie it into everything else at the same time, that is to say, work on one feature at a time.
  • Don't plan too far ahead. You'll end up pressuring yourself to do those things, even if it doesn't make sense or if I don't want to.

With these lessons, I decided to only work on a cooking demo, and literally nothing else. By that I mean I only plan on adding things that will contribute towards implementing my cooking system. As such, systems such as movement or combat will be completely unnecessary (for now). That way, I can focus fully on developing the system and making sure that I can fully understand how it works and what it requires.

To start off, I plan on working on the UI and basic inventory, since you need it to access your ingredients. I'll specifically work on developing an IngredientSlot class, to show what ingredients the user has.

That'll be it for today. I just wanted to explain why I'm moving on and what I plan to do now. Thank you, and have a good day/night!

~Osmond "TheOneAndOlee" Lee
