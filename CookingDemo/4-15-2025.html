Small Update: A New Game Engine!

I just wanted to update you all on my decision to move from Unity to Godot!

]

I originally started with Unity because I had a class in it. After dropping the Space Invaders project, I had time to think about what game engine I'd want to use. I knew after watching more than a few videos that Unity was definitely not the move. The corporation probably will do some scummy tactics to screw over its users, and I did not want that.

From there, I decided to look into free and open-source alternatives. I had a few requirements:

  • It had a decently large community, meaning the forums and community itself are very active.
  • It doesn't have an atrocious learning curve.
  • I could program in C# or C++.
  • The game engine has good 2D game development support.

Knowing all of that, there really are only two options for me: MonoGame and Godot. Between those two, Godot is by far the more appealing choice. From what I saw, it allows for much faster development since I didn't have to worry about low-level resource management like what I saw in MonoGame. I also already had some very limited experience with Godot's node system, so it was a no-brainer to choose it.

To end it, I will say that I'll definitely be busier for the next month, since it's the last month of class, and I got a lot tests to study for and a Blender project that's due soon. Though if I have free time, I will definitely try and see what I can do in order to develop my Godot skills. Thank you for reading, and have a good day/night!

~Osmond "TheOneAndOlee" Lee
